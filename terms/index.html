Privacy Policy

Your privacy and data are important to us. Our privacy policy is as follows:

Information we collect

Through the Smallchat Slack integration, we access and store information about your team and team’s members for the purpose of displaying that information to visitors you chat with. That information includes but is not limited to full names and profile pictures. We also have access to and may store, but do not display to visitors, data available through the Slack OAuth scopes that Smallchat accesses. The data available through these scopes can be reviewed in Slack’s API documentation.

We may access and store email addresses from users of the Service. We do not sell those email addresses but may use them to send information about Smallchat, maintenance, changes to Smallchat, and new features.

We process message exchanges between the Smallchat customer and end-user. This includes publicly available information on the end-user: browser name and version, operating device type, and activity time. We collect but do not display the end-user’s public IP address, for security, and to provide the Smallchat customer a method to prevent further messages from the end-user. The public IP address is also used to guess the end-user’s city and country location to assist the customer in helping the end-user.

Any other data collected on a Smallchat customer’s end-users is the responsibility of that Smallchat customer to manage. It is our responsibility to secure access to this data.

Information we share

Smallchat does not share any personal data, for advertising or any other purpose.

Privacy communication

We are committed to ensuring that the confidentiality of personal information is protected and maintained. Smallchat customers should include their own terms of use and privacy policy. We will make readily available to customers information about our policies and practices relating to the management of personal information.

Consent

Smallchat only processes customer data after receiving explicit consent via Slack authorization. We also process end-user-submitted data in message exchanges. Any data collected with Smallchat’s Javascript API must be done lawfully and with the end-user’s consent.

GDPR

We’ve documented our steps to become GDPR compliant. For more information on the following steps, see https://ico.org.uk/media/1624219/preparing-for-the-gdpr-12-steps.pdf

Awareness

All Nicer LLC employees working on Smallchat are aware of GDPR requirements and informed about our steps to be compliant.

Information we hold

Smallchat collects personal data solely for the purpose of providing the service, and only with the consent of the user. Smallchat processes the following types of personal data:

  • Slack user first and last name
  • Slack user email address
  • Slack user profile
  • Slack team profile
  • Message exchanges between Slack user and end-user
  • Activity date and time
  • End-user name (if provided)
  • End-user email address (if provided)
  • End-user IP address (used for security, and to provide a method to prevent messages from an end-user)
  • End-user device type (operating system and browser)
  • End-user city and country (guessed from the IP address)

Individuals’ rights

Smallchat upholds our customers’ rights regarding GDPR:

  • Right to be informed: we are transparent about how we use your data.
  • Right of access: our users can request access to all their data from Smallchat
  • Right of rectification: our users can request any personal data be rectified if it is inaccurate or incomplete.
  • Right to restrict processing: we don't process the data of our customers or our customers’ end-users.
  • Right of erasure: our users can request to have personal data erased.
  • it's as simple as contacting us, we'll process all your erasure queries
  • Right to data portability: our users can request an export of their personal data.
  • Right to object: our users can contact us at any time with regards to this matter.
  • Rights related to automation & profiling: we do not use your data for automation or profiling.

Subject access requests

We will respond within 30 days to support requests about users’ rights.

Lawful basis for processing personal data

Smallchat obtains personal data from your Slack team after explicit consent when you add Smallchat to your Slack account. It is your responsibility as a Smallchat customer to ensure any data you otherwise collect through Smallchat messages is obtained lawfully.

Children

Smallchat requires a minimum age of 16. This is indirectly enforced due to our integration with Slack. You must have a Slack account in order to set up a Smallchat account, and Slack also requires a minimum age of 16. Children may interact as end-users by using the Smallchat messenger. In this case it is the Smallchat customer’s responsibility to uphold GDPR regulations regarding children.
